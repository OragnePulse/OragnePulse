Cookie Regulation Policy

Last updated: December 2, 2025

This Cookie Regulation Policy explains how Oragné Pulse ("we", "us", "our") uses cookies and similar technologies on our website. This is done to improve performance, provide necessary functionality, and personalize your experience. By continuing to browse our site, you consent to our use of cookies as described in this policy.

2. What are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files placed on your device (computer, tablet, or mobile phone) when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work efficiently, as well as to provide information to the owners of the site.

3. How We Use Cookies

We use cookies for various purposes categorized as follows:

  • Essential Cookies: Required for the operation of our website, enabling core functions like security, network management, and accessibility. These cannot be switched off.
  • Performance and Analytics Cookies: Allow us to recognize and count the number of visitors and see how visitors move around our website. This helps us to improve the way our website works.
  • Functionality Cookies: Used to recognize you when you return to our website, enabling us to personalize our content for you and remember your preferences (e.g., language or region).
  • Advertising and Marketing Cookies: Record your visit to our website, the pages you have visited, and the links you have followed. We will use this information to make our website and the advertising displayed on it more relevant to your interests.

4. Types of Cookies We Use

Cookies can be further distinguished by origin and duration:

  • First-Party Cookies: Set by the website you are visiting (Oragné Pulse).
  • Third-Party Cookies: Set by a domain other than the one of the website you are visiting (e.g., Google Analytics, social media sites).
  • Session Cookies: Temporarily stored during a browsing session and are deleted when you close the browser.
  • Persistent Cookies: Stored on your device between browser sessions to remember your preferences or actions across the site.

5. Managing Your Preferences

You have the right to decide whether to accept or reject cookies. You can exercise your cookie preferences by:

  • Adjusting the settings in your web browser to refuse all or some cookies.
  • Using our dedicated cookie consent tool or banner, where applicable, to manage non-essential cookies.

Please note that if you choose to block essential cookies, parts of our website may not function correctly.

5.1 Browser-Specific Guidance

To manage cookies directly through your browser, please consult the relevant guidance below:

  • Chrome: Managing Cookies and site data
  • Firefox: Clear cookies and site data in Firefox
  • Edge: Delete and manage cookies
  • Safari: Manage cookies and website data
